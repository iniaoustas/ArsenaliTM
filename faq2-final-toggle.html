What is a Tango Marathon? Marathon vs Festival Registration Tickets & Refunds Etiquette Role balance Schedule Venue Photos & Media Privacy/GDPR

A Tango Marathon is a multi-day social dance event focused on continuous milongas (usually 3–4 days) with invited DJs and few or no classes or shows. Many events aim for a friendly, balanced, “retreat-like” atmosphere.

Festivals typically include workshops, shows and live music. Marathons focus on social dancing across long milonga blocks; some may add small extras, but dancing remains the core.

Registration is required; most marathons curate a guest list and aim for balanced roles (leaders/followers), sometimes allowing a small variance. Couple registration may be offered.

Policies vary by organizer. A number of marathons use limited or no-refund policies and often make tickets non-transferable after confirmation; others allow name changes before a deadline. Always check the event’s terms.

We encourage inviting by mirada–cabeceo, respecting clear consent, and dancing safely (floor-craft). Organizers typically prohibit harassment or discrimination and may enforce a code of conduct.

Expect long milonga blocks (afternoon & night) across several days, usually with short off-hours for rest. Details and DJs are on the Schedule section of our site.

The marathon takes place at Μεγάλο Αρσενάλι (Megalo Arsenal), Michail Afentoulief, Chania 731 32. See the Venue section or open navigation from our map embed.

Many marathons include snack bars or light meals to support the “retreat-like” vibe; specifics vary by event and pass type—check our Tickets section when published.

Many events inform participants that photos/video may be captured and used for promotion; opt-out routes may exist (e.g., contacting the organizers). Our GDPR policy explains how we handle media and personal data.

We collect only the data necessary for registration and communication and retain it for a limited time. See our full GDPR & Privacy Policy for rights and contact details.
