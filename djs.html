Meet the DJs

Short bios, highlights and links

her heart belongs to tango

Tonia Papadaki

Fri 22:00–03:00

Tonia Papadaki is a doctor and lives in Chania but her heart belongs to tango. She started dancing on 2007 but she wanted more… So on 2010 she started TJing and learning more and more about tango music. Since then she has been invited to play in many milongas and festivals in Greece. Her choices in music are characterized by diversity of rhythms and melodies of the Golden Age but eventually she also loves to pick up a few Tandas from the modern orchestras as well.

every tanda tells a story

Jan Tango

Sat 16:00–20:00

A passionate DJ and tango dancer for many years, Jan shares his love for music through refined and heartfelt selections. He carefully crafts tandas that honor the energy of the orchestras and the flow of the milonga, keeping dancers connected at every moment. His style is defined by great attention to sound quality, deep respect for the tradition of Argentine tango, and an artistic sensitivity rooted in his musical background. In Chania, he will present balanced sets—both intense and elegant—where every tanda tells a story.

Babis Pappas

Sun 18:00–22:00

Babis makes sure to create the ideal musical atmosphere, focusing on creating a dynamic and emotional experience for the dancers, combining traditional and modern musical selections that bring out the authentic character of tango. With his deep understanding of the genre’s history and styles, he builds the ideal musical journey, tailored to the needs of each night and audience. It aims to keep the flow alive and the passion of the dance undiminished, offering dancers an evening full of tension, emotional connection and true enjoyment

Mert Emre Üstün

Fri 16:00–20:00

During this time, he has performed at milongas and events in cities such as Drama, Komotini, Xanthi, and Alexandroupoli, becoming a prominent figure in the regional tango community. feels especially close to the sound of the bandoneón

Michal Shevchenko

Thu 22:00–02:00

Michał Shevchenko, Director of the Tango Museum, DJ and Vinyl DJ with experience playing across Europe and in Buenos Aires. He has performed at major festivals and marathons in Poland, Spain, Italy, Germany, the United Kingdom, the Czech Republic, and Ukraine. He has had the honor of playing at iconic milongas in Buenos Aires, including Gente Amiga and Maldita Milonga. He runs the Tango Museum and Salon as well as Milonga de Salon in the south of Poland, in Bielsko-Biała. He loves the shifting dynamics of Aníbal Troilo, the lyricism of Carlos Di Sarli, the energy of Juan D’Arienzo, and the baroque style of Osvaldo Pugliese. He feels especially close to the sound of the bandoneón, which he is learning to play. Favorite piece? It depends on the time of day and the day of the week :) Today it would be A. Troilo’s El bulín de la calle Ayacucho.

to animate, to create joy, to spark connections

Santiago Buonomo

Sat 22:00–03:00

Crete is one of my favourite places on earth, so being part of this marathon is not only an honour but also a joy that touches me deeply. I began DJing in Uruguay in 2014, playing in some of the country’s most iconic milongas, such as El Chamuyo, Joven Tango, Lo de Margot, and La Callejera. Since then, my journey has taken me across Europe, where I’ve played in numerous milongas, festivals, and marathons, always staying true to the classics of the Golden Age. Along the way, I’ve also organized events such as Milonga Charrúa y Bailarín in Kraków, the Gdynia Tango Festival in Poland, and Tango Needs Your Style. My DJ style is rooted in the tradition of tango: tandas that keep the floor alive with the energy of the Golden Age, and cortinas that remind us we’re not only at a dance, but at a celebration. Everywhere I go, I bring the same dream I had since childhood—to animate, to create joy, to spark connections. And to do that here, in Crete, makes it all the more special.

just ... be niaou

John Niaou

Sun 14:00–18:00

Giannis Niaoustas (aka John “El Gato” Niaou) lives and works in Chania. He joined the Tango world, somewhere in 2009, when he decided to enroll in a local dance school in Chania to “be initiated” into the secrets of Tango. Following years until today are a story -almost- of dedication to our beloved dance. For about 6 years, he taught Tango at the same school where he was taught, participated in performances and seminars and was an organizer of milongas and many Tango events. At the same time, he was involved in TDj-ing performing in milongas and festivals He mainly chooses Golden Age orchestras, however, he enriches his program with contemporary musical groups that maintain the style of the era. Rhythm and melody dominate his tandas and the dance floor is on fire. Favorite musicians Juan d’Arienzo and Rodolfo Biagi.
